Shiro

Description

Shiro is a traditional Ethiopian dish made from powdered chickpeas or broad bean flour, cooked with spices and sometimes mixed with onions, garlic, and berbere (a spice blend). It is typically served as a thick, flavorful stew that is enjoyed with injera, a type of sourdough flatbread. Shiro is a staple in Ethiopian cuisine and is loved for its rich taste and comforting texture.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups shiro powder (chickpea or broad bean flour)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons berbere spice mix
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced butter)
  • 4 cups water or vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil or niter kibbeh in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they are soft and translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the berbere spice mix and cook for 1-2 minutes to release its flavors.
  5. Gradually add the shiro powder, stirring constantly to prevent lumps.
  6. Slowly pour in the water or vegetable broth while continuously stirring to create a smooth mixture.
  7. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens to a stew-like consistency.
  8. Season with salt to taste.
  9. Serve hot with injera or your favorite flatbread.
